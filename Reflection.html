Reflection


During the process of writing my multigenre project, I was mostly surprised at the new stuff I learned. The problem of education funding is even deeper than I initially thought, being that it affects minorities disproportionately, which leads to a host of other problems for them. I was also surprised with how good my argumentative essay turned out. I am really proud of the way I used evidence to support my points thoroughly. Additionally, throughout this project, I learned how to write in different genres as a way of inquiring into my topic of choice to communicate my knowledge. I wrote an argumentative essay, an editorial article, an editorial cartoon, and a comic strip. I feel like I learned how much info to provide in each. With the essay, I could go very deep into the topic, explaining a ton of information to support my argument. With the editorial, I provided less information, sticking more to important points. And with the comic and the editorial cartoon a lot less information was given. The information I gave was simply for the punchline in the comic and a lot of symbolism for the editorial cartoon. I think that the strongest parts of my project were my argumentative essay and my editorial article. Therefore, following that train of thought, I think that my editorial cartoon and comic strip were my weakest parts. I think this because I am more comfortable with the mediums of an argumentative essay or article then cartoons or comics. I am not great at art, so I think I can communicate information better with writing than with images. As a result of this, I think my writings were better. Finally, I think that I deserve an A on this project. I put a lot of effort into the research of my topic, I spent hours reading through every source, I took a lot of notes, and I combined this information into a series of pieces that reflect my knowledge and deliver a message to the reader in an effective manner.
